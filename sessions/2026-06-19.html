Big Question

What makes someone a Christian?

Is it going to church? Reading the Bible? Being a good person? Praying? Not swearing? Or is it something deeper?

✦ Most people can recognize a Christian stereotype. It is much harder to explain what actually makes someone a Christian.
Identity

A Christian is someone who follows Jesus.

"If anyone would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross daily and follow me." Luke 9:23
Relationship Growth Following Jesus

The word Christian originally meant "little Christ." It was first used in Antioch because people noticed that Jesus' followers lived differently.

Not perfection

Christians still make mistakes, struggle with sin, and need forgiveness. Following Jesus does not mean never failing.

Relationship

Christianity is first about knowing and following Jesus, not simply following a list of rules.

Growth

Following Jesus changes us over time. God cares about our hearts and our direction, not just our behaviour.

✦ Fun fact: The word "Christian" appears only 3 times in the Bible, but "disciple" appears more than 250 times. Jesus spent much more time talking about following Him than about labels.

Discussion: What is the difference between believing in Jesus and following Jesus?

Real Life

Can someone be Christian and still swear?

Some people say yes

Christians are not perfect. Everyone struggles with sin and still needs God's grace.

Some people say no

Following Jesus should gradually change how we speak, think, and treat others.

The bigger question may not be whether a Christian has ever sworn. The bigger question is: Are they becoming more like Jesus over time?

"Let no corrupting talk come out of your mouths..." Ephesians 4:29

Paul wrote Ephesians to Christians in the city of Ephesus. His point was not simply "don't say bad words." His point was that our words can either build people up or tear people down.

✦ Discussion: Is swearing always wrong? Does the intent behind our words matter?

Church

Can you be Christian and not go to church?

"Not neglecting to meet together, as is the habit of some..." Hebrews 10:25

Yes

Going to church does not save someone or make them a Christian.

But...

God designed Christians to grow together, learn together, and support one another.

Going to church does not make someone a Christian any more than standing in a garage makes someone a car. But Christians were never meant to follow Jesus completely alone.

✦ Acts 2 describes the first Christians meeting regularly for teaching, prayer, meals, worship, and helping one another.
Discussion:
  • Why do you think God wants Christians to gather together?
  • Can someone follow Jesus alone?
  • What makes church helpful? What makes church difficult?
Corner Debate

A Christian should look different from everyone else.

Move to one side of the room. Be ready to explain why.

Strongly Agree

Christians should clearly stand out from culture.

Agree

Some things should look different, but not everything.

Disagree

Christians can look very similar to everyone else.

Strongly Disagree

Faith is mostly internal and not always visible.

"Do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewal of your mind." Romans 12:2
✦ Jesus often looked completely ordinary. People noticed Him because of how He loved, taught, and treated others.
Discussion:
  • What should look different about a Christian?
  • Behaviour?
  • Language?
  • Social media?
  • Values and priorities?
  • Can someone follow Jesus and still look similar to everyone else?
Identity

What does a Christian actually look like?

A Christian is not someone who is perfect. A Christian is someone who trusts Jesus and is learning to follow Him.

Not Perfect

Christians still make mistakes and need God's grace.

Following Jesus

Being Christian is about a relationship with Jesus, not simply rules.

Growing

Change often happens slowly over time.

Different

People may notice kindness, humility, forgiveness, and love.

"By this all people will know that you are my disciples, if you have love for one another." John 13:35
✦ Jesus said people would recognize His followers by their love, not by their perfection.
Discussion:
  • Who is someone that lives like Jesus?
  • What qualities stand out?
  • Would your friends know you follow Jesus?
  • How would they know?
